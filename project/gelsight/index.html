IrisTac

IrisTac has proposed a novel tactile sensor design, which utilizes a multicolored LED ring to simulate various lighting environments. This design employs a single sensor platform to control variables and investigate the impact of lighting conditions on surface reconstruction of objects.

In this study, I was involved in the entire process from initial research and idea implementation to experimental validation and paper writing. Starting with limited knowledge, I conducted extensive literature review to gradually identify innovative points. Subsequently, I was responsible for building the sensor, which involved hardware selection, enclosure modeling, and algorithm design. Finally, experiments were designed and conducted to validate the findings.

Technology stack:

Building the electromechanical system of the sensor: PCB design (Iceda), sensor enclosure design (Fusion360), basic image processing (OpenCV), machine learning algorithm (photometric stereo algorithm).

ability :

Basic knowledge of tactile sensors; Ability to read and analyze paper; Understanding the standard research process

Achievements :

Publishing a paper at an international conference as a secondary author
